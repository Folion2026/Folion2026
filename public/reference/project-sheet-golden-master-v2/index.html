Fennell Bay Master Plan

Fennell Bay, NSW 2021 Landcom
Project Description

Fennell Bay Master Plan establishes a framework for a new residential community embedded within the site's substantial bushland, ridgeline and creek systems. The proposal responds to a complex landscape shaped by biodiversity, topography, heritage, bushfire risk, flooding and former mining activity, using these conditions to structure rather than constrain the design.

Organised around Marmong Creek, retained woodland, blue-green corridors and three walkable micro-neighbourhoods, the master plan combines housing diversity with active transport, public open space and ecological conservation. A Community Hub and park form the social heart of the plan, while walking and cycling networks strengthen connections to surrounding centres, rail stations and Lake Macquarie.

Innovation

The master plan turns ecological constraints into the organising structure of the community. Marmong Creek, retained woodland, topography and blue-green corridors define neighbourhood edges, movement networks and open space, creating a development framework where landscape performance and place identity are inseparable.

Lessons Learned

Complex greenfield sites benefit from treating technical constraints as design intelligence early in the process. Aligning ecology, topography, access and community infrastructure from the outset can reduce conflict between disciplines and produce a clearer, more resilient structure for future stages of design.
